about the history of kolkata

Kolkata, formerly known as Calcutta, holds a rich history. Established in the late 17th century as a trading post by the British East India Company, it became India's capital during the British colonial era. Kolkata played a pivotal role in the Indian independence movement, witnessing significant events such as the Partition of Bengal in 1905 and the Jallianwala Bagh massacre in 1919. This city was home to luminaries like Rabindranath Tagore and Subhas Chandra Bose and was a hub of the Swadeshi Movement. Kolkata's cultural heritage, seen in the Bengal Renaissance, has left a lasting impact, shaping literature, art, and education. Today, the city embodies a harmonious blend of tradition and modernity, reflecting its dynamic history.
